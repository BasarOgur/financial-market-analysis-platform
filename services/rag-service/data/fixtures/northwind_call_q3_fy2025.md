Chief Executive Officer, prepared remarks:

Thank you, and good morning everyone. Our third quarter results reflect steady execution in a value-conscious consumer environment. Comparable sales grew 1.8%, our sixth consecutive quarter of positive comps, and we gained share in beauty, pet, and private-label grocery.

Our loyalty program, Northwind Rewards, reached 41 million active members, up 12% year over year. Rewards members now account for roughly two-thirds of total sales and shop with us 40% more frequently than non-members, which underpins our personalization and retail media roadmap.

Looking ahead to the holiday quarter, we are guiding fourth quarter net sales to a range of $10.2 billion to $10.6 billion, with comparable sales of flat to up 2%. We expect fourth quarter gross margin to expand modestly year over year as freight tailwinds continue, though we are planning promotional intensity consistent with last year.

Chief Financial Officer, prepared remarks:

Capital expenditures were $280 million in the quarter, and we continue to expect approximately $1.1 billion for the full year, weighted toward supply chain automation and the next-generation store remodel program.

We repurchased $220 million of shares in the quarter and have $500 million remaining under our current authorization. Our leverage ratio ended the quarter at 1.9 times adjusted debt to EBITDAR, within our target range.

Q&A session (excerpt):

Analyst: Can you unpack the drivers of the fourth quarter guidance range?

CFO: The low end assumes holiday traffic consistent with the last two months, and the high end assumes modest acceleration in discretionary categories, particularly electronics and toys. We have not assumed any improvement in big-ticket home categories, which remain under pressure.

Analyst: How should we think about shrink into next year?

CFO: Shrink remains a headwind of roughly 30 basis points versus two years ago. Our mitigation investments are showing results in pilot stores, and we expect shrink to be approximately neutral to gross margin in fiscal 2026.
