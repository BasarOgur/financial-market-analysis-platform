Liquidity and Capital Resources

As of the end of the second quarter of fiscal 2025, we had $1.9 billion in cash and cash equivalents and $340 million in short-term investments. Our $750 million senior revolving credit facility, which matures in fiscal 2028, remained undrawn at quarter end.

Cash used in operations was $180 million for the quarter, reflecting a $410 million increase in working capital driven by inventory staged for second-half deliveries and milestone-based billing terms on two large utility projects. We expect working capital to unwind and operating cash flow to turn positive in the fourth quarter as these projects reach delivery milestones.

Our 1.25% convertible senior notes, with an aggregate principal amount of $600 million, mature in fiscal 2027. We intend to settle the principal amount in cash and are evaluating refinancing alternatives, including a new convertible issuance or drawing on committed project-level credit facilities.

Capital expenditures were $260 million in the quarter, of which $190 million related to construction of our Nevada battery cell manufacturing facility. We expect total capital expenditures of approximately $1.0 billion for the full fiscal year, with the Nevada facility representing roughly 60% of the total. Committed equipment purchases for the facility total $420 million over the next four quarters.

We believe our existing cash, investments, undrawn revolver capacity, and expected operating cash flows will be sufficient to fund our operations, capital expenditures, and debt service obligations for at least the next twelve months. A sustained deterioration in project payment timing, or an inability to refinance the convertible notes on acceptable terms, could require us to reduce planned capacity investments.
