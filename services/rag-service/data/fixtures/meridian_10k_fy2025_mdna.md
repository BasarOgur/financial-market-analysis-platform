Management's Discussion and Analysis of Financial Condition and Results of Operations

Overview. Meridian Semiconductors designs and sells high-performance analog and mixed-signal processors for data center power management, automotive electrification, and industrial automation. We operate a fabless model and rely on third-party foundries for wafer fabrication.

Results of Operations. Total revenue for fiscal year 2025 was $4.82 billion, an increase of 18.1% compared to $4.08 billion in fiscal year 2024. The increase was driven primarily by strength in our Data Center segment, partially offset by continued softness in Industrial.

Data Center segment revenue was $2.10 billion, an increase of 41% year over year, reflecting accelerating deployment of our power-stage controllers in AI server racks. Our top hyperscale customers expanded orders across two consecutive product generations during the year.

Automotive segment revenue was $1.31 billion, an increase of 6% year over year, as content gains in battery management systems offset lower unit volumes at two European OEM customers. Industrial segment revenue was $1.41 billion, a decrease of 4% year over year, reflecting distributor inventory digestion that began in the second quarter.

Gross margin was 58.2% in fiscal 2025 compared to 55.9% in fiscal 2024. The 230 basis point improvement was driven by a richer data center product mix and improved foundry pricing under our renegotiated long-term supply agreement, partially offset by higher advanced-packaging costs.

Research and development expense was $980 million, or 20.3% of revenue, compared to $840 million in the prior year, reflecting increased headcount in our power-architecture design centers and higher tape-out costs on the 5-nanometer node.

Operating income was $1.02 billion, representing an operating margin of 21.2%, compared to $760 million and 18.6% in fiscal 2024.

Liquidity. We ended fiscal 2025 with $2.3 billion in cash, cash equivalents and short-term investments. Cash generated from operations was $1.34 billion. Capital expenditures were $210 million, principally for test and validation equipment. We do not currently pay a dividend; our board has authorized management to retain earnings to fund product development and selective capacity prepayments to foundry partners.
