Quarterly Financial Review

Net sales for the third quarter of fiscal 2025 were $8.6 billion, an increase of 3.2% compared to $8.33 billion in the third quarter of fiscal 2024. Comparable store sales increased 1.8%, driven by a 2.6% increase in average transaction value, partially offset by a 0.8% decline in transaction count.

Digital commerce net sales grew 14% year over year and represented 28% of total net sales in the quarter, up from 25% in the prior-year quarter. Approximately 62% of digital orders were fulfilled from stores, including curbside pickup and ship-from-store.

Gross profit rate was 34.1% of net sales, an increase of 60 basis points from the prior-year quarter, reflecting lower ocean freight rates and reduced clearance markdowns, partially offset by continued investment in everyday price positioning and higher shrink.

Selling, general and administrative expenses were $2.41 billion, or 28.0% of net sales, compared to 28.3% in the prior-year quarter, as store labor productivity initiatives offset wage rate increases.

Merchandise inventory at quarter end was $6.1 billion, a decrease of 9% compared to the third quarter of fiscal 2024, reflecting disciplined receipts and faster inventory turns in apparel and home categories.

During the quarter we opened 14 new stores and closed 23 stores, ending the period with 1,842 stores. Remodel activity was completed in 96 locations under our next-generation store format.

Our board of directors declared a quarterly cash dividend of $0.62 per share, payable in the fourth quarter. Year-to-date we have returned $1.9 billion to shareholders through dividends and share repurchases.
