Chief Executive Officer, prepared remarks:

Good afternoon. Helios delivered another strong quarter across both our utility-scale solar and energy storage businesses. Total installations were 2.4 gigawatts in the quarter, up 26% year over year, and revenue was $2.9 billion, up 22%.

Energy storage continues to be our fastest-growing product line. Our storage attach rate — the share of new solar projects contracted with co-located battery storage — reached 38% in the quarter, up from 29% a year ago. We believe attach rates above 50% are achievable within two years as interconnection queues increasingly favor dispatchable projects.

Our contracted backlog grew to $6.8 billion at quarter end, providing visibility into deliveries through fiscal 2027. Roughly 70% of backlog includes indexed pricing provisions that pass through commodity cost movements.

On supply chain, domestic content across our module supply reached 55% this quarter, and we remain on track to exceed 70% next fiscal year as our Nevada battery cell facility ramps in the second half. The Nevada ramp is the primary driver of our expected storage gross margin expansion, as in-house cells replace imported cells that currently carry tariff exposure.

For the full fiscal year, we are raising our installation guidance to a range of 9.5 to 10.0 gigawatts, up from our prior range of 9.0 to 9.6 gigawatts, and we now expect full-year revenue growth of approximately 20%.

I want to be clear about interest rate sensitivity, because we get this question often: roughly 80% of our utility-scale pipeline is with investment-grade utility counterparties under long-term power purchase agreements, and project financing is typically secured at contract signing, which limits our direct exposure to rate movements between signing and delivery.
