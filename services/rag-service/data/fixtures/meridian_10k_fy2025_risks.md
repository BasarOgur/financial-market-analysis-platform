Risk Factors (selected excerpts)

Customer concentration. Our revenue is concentrated among a small number of customers. In fiscal 2025, our two largest customers together accounted for 31% of total revenue, and our ten largest customers accounted for 58% of total revenue. The loss of, or a significant reduction in orders from, either of our two largest customers would materially and adversely affect our results of operations. Neither customer is obligated to purchase minimum quantities, and both may cancel or reschedule orders on short notice.

Export controls and China exposure. Sales to customers in China represented 22% of our total revenue in fiscal 2025. Evolving U.S. export control regulations applicable to advanced semiconductors may restrict our ability to sell certain data center power-management products to Chinese customers, or may require licenses that we may not obtain in a timely manner or at all. Further tightening of these regulations could materially reduce our addressable market in China.

Foundry dependence. We depend on a single advanced-node foundry partner for substantially all wafers used in our Data Center segment products. We do not have guaranteed capacity, and any disruption at our foundry partner — including natural disaster, geopolitical conflict affecting Taiwan, or reallocation of capacity to larger customers — could prevent us from meeting customer demand for an extended period. Qualifying an alternative foundry for our most advanced products would take at least eighteen months.

Inventory obsolescence. Demand for our products is difficult to forecast, particularly in the data center market where customer architectures change rapidly between product generations. If demand shifts away from products we have already manufactured, we may be required to record significant inventory write-downs. In fiscal 2025 we recorded $48 million of inventory reserves, compared to $31 million in fiscal 2024.

Competition. We compete against larger vertically integrated semiconductor companies with greater manufacturing scale and pricing flexibility. Sustained pricing pressure in the automotive battery-management market reduced our average selling prices in that segment by approximately 3% during fiscal 2025.
